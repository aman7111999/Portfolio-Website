<Section id="problem" eyebrow="01 · Problem" title="A launchpad, not a home.">

The old dashboard was a wall of links to features. New merchants bounced. Power users built their own workflows outside it. We rebuilt the surface around the five jobs merchants actually did every week.

<Callout kind="warning" title="The bounce">
43% of new merchants never returned after signing up. Interviews confirmed the reason: the dashboard was optimized for the org chart, not the user. Nothing on the home screen told a merchant what to do next.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section id="research" eyebrow="02 · Research" title="One number, one alert, one next step.">

We shadowed 12 merchants for a week each. Every session ended with the same finding: the merchant needed one number, one alert, and one next step. Everything else was noise pretending to be signal.

</Section>

<PullQuote author="Merchant interview, week 3">
I don't need a dashboard. I need to know what I'm supposed to do today.
</PullQuote>

<Section id="approach" eyebrow="03 · Approach" title="Headline, queue, tray.">

<Steps items={[
  { title: "Headline metric", body: "One number that changes with the merchant's stage — GMV, refund rate, or a compliance signal." },
  { title: "Action queue",    body: "Driven by anomalies and opportunities, not features. Merchants triage instead of navigating." },
  { title: "System tray",     body: "Everything else — quiet, discoverable, keyboard-navigable for power users." }
]} />

Density scales with maturity — pros unlock more, beginners see less.

</Section>

<Section id="system" eyebrow="04 · Home surface" title="A pattern the whole product could reuse.">

The 'headline · queue · tray' pattern is now the shared vocabulary for every merchant surface we design. It gave the team a way to talk about density and progression instead of arguing pixel by pixel.

<Callout kind="learning" title="Lesson">
Dashboards fail when they answer 'what can I do?' instead of 'what should I do?'. Every good product has a point of view about the next best action.
</Callout>

<Divider label="Impact" />

</Section>

<Section id="impact" eyebrow="05 · Impact" title="A quieter surface earned more attention.">

Time to first meaningful action fell by more than half. Weekly active merchants rose 18% within a quarter. Support tickets originating from the home surface dropped 44%.

</Section>

<Section id="reflection" eyebrow="06 · Reflection" title="Restraint as a strategy.">

The hardest work was cutting — arguing against the twelve teams whose surface deserved a home tile "if you're being fair." Being fair to teams and being useful to users are different jobs.

</Section>
