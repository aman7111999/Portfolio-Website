<Section id="problem" eyebrow="01 · Problem" title="Excellent research nobody could find.">

Reports lived in Notion, decks lived in Drive, insights lived in slack threads. In interviews, 8 out of 10 PMs said they'd "probably re-run research that already existed." The cost was not just wasted studies — it was decisions made without prior context.

</Section>

<Section id="approach" eyebrow="02 · Approach" title="Insights, not reports, as the atomic unit.">

Each insight has a claim, a source, a confidence level, and a tag graph. The home surface is a query builder disguised as search: "insights about onboarding, high confidence, from the last 6 months."

<Callout kind="decision" title="The atomic unit">
Reports are containers; insights are the currency. Design the currency first, and the containers become optional.
</Callout>

<Divider label="Impact" />

</Section>

<Section id="impact" eyebrow="03 · Impact" title="A network, not a service desk.">

Weekly viewers grew to nearly 200 people across three functions. PRDs began linking research inline. The research team stopped feeling like a service desk and started acting like a knowledge network.

</Section>

<Section id="reflection" eyebrow="04 · Reflection" title="Internal tools are the highest-leverage design work most companies never invest in.">

The ROI on a good one is measured in years. This was one of the projects I'm most proud of, and the least visible outside the company.

</Section>
